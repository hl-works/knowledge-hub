How I use Claude

Read the full guide →

The core of my method comes down to a few .md files I paste into Claude's "Project Instructions." A main CLAUDE.md that defines my tone, rules, and context; extension files loaded on demand (strategy, dev…); and a dedicated file for the DNA of each brand.

The guiding principle: Compound Engineering. Every time Claude gets my style wrong, I add a rule. The system improves with use — and all future work benefits from it.

    Customer order status: invoiced, paid, delivered — three badges instead of one

    Nothing clearly distinguished "the invoice has been sent," "the customer has paid," and "the goods have been delivered." Three independent badges (Invoicing / Payment / Delivery) replace the merged status: color coding, deposit percentage displayed, automatic switch from amount to collect → amount to deliver, delivered lines grayed out. Ported from the supplier module already in place; two latent calculation bugs fixed along the way.

Two incidents (an overwritten version, a cron missing its environment variables) recovered in minutes thanks to the save/compare discipline before every deployment.

  • 2026-07-12

    Overhauling the Odoo pricing system (cobra_price): one single source of truth

    The Odoo pricing model was layered and fragile: auto-calculated web pricelist, stacked price lists, 20% VAT hardcoded, margin dropping to 0 whenever a product went out of stock. A single cobra_price field becomes THE source of truth via a _price_compute override — cost and margin recalculated, Omnibus-compliant strikethrough prices, immediate Shopify sync. 6 modules touched, ~7,300 products migrated, with real post-prod follow-up (display bug fixed the same day, 492 products on sale pushed back).

    odoo-devshopifydataen-prod
    Claude · Cobra
  • 2026-07-12

    An integrator code review handled in full, point by point

    After Cobra modified an Odoo module, the integrator (Irokoo) came back with 6 technical points (security, reliability, consistency). Each one evaluated individually: 3 fixed as-is, 2 with an explicit judgment call (keeping the chatter visibility Hugo wanted while neutralizing the risk another way), 1 deliberately set aside. A bug Irokoo hadn't flagged was caught and fixed along the way. Deployed in an isolated Git worktree to avoid interfering with another ongoing workstream.

  • 2026-07-11

    A homegrown competitive pricing dashboard, instead of an integrator quote

    The most straightforward build vs buy case of the period: a spec for a competitive pricing dashboard (ABC scoring, rules engine, price history), estimated at 3–4 weeks of dev, sent to the integrator in March 2026 — sitting at "to validate before development," never kicked off. The most technical piece (import/matching of prices via Wiser) was already running in prod; the control layer was missing. Built in-house with Claude Code, in a few days, on our internal app hub.

  • 2026-07-11

    A €200,000 supplier backlog cleared in one week, line by line

    A statement from a major supplier (GDD) showed a significant outstanding balance spread across dozens of invoices — and didn't always reflect the latest payments on the Odoo side, creating a risk of double payment. Systematic reconciliation of supplier statement / Odoo / bank statement with each new statement, six payment batches tracked one by one through to actual bank confirmation: €200,059.33 settled in 6 wire transfers, two discrepancies (−€60, +€18) flagged to the supplier for correction.
